About Me

A curious kid from Puerto Rico who never stopped taking things apart.

The Short Version

I started with a soldering iron and a radio I probably shouldn't have opened. That spark of curiosity turned into an electronics degree, then a career in software, and eventually a life spent making things — code, music, circuits, and whatever weird idea I woke up thinking about.

I've been writing software for over 20 years, but the job title is the least interesting part. What I really do is build stuff — tools, sounds, gadgets, experiences. If it can be created, I want to try.

What Gets Me Going

The feeling when a beat drops right. The smell of rosin on a fresh solder joint. That moment a side project goes from "this is broken" to "wait… this actually works." I live for the creative process — messy, surprising, and never boring.

When I'm not at my desk, I'm probably noodling on a synth, prototyping some half-baked gadget, or deep in a rabbit hole about something I didn't know existed yesterday. Life's too short to stop learning.
